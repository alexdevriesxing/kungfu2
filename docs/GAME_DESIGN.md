# Game design bible

## High concept

**Kung Fu: Return of the Green Dragon Master** is a side-scrolling wuxia JRPG where investigation and relationships unfold through free-roaming villages, roads, monasteries and schools, while major confrontations become fast, readable arcade duels. It combines the social clarity of a JRPG with the immediacy of a dedicated martial-arts fighting game.

## Story premise

Shi-An returns to Green Dragon Hall and finds Master Shen murdered, the school burned and the master's jade sword missing. The official story blames Red Sash bandits. Witnesses remember details that do not fit: a heel-less executioner's step, temple ink, wintergreen oil and a silent Shaolin bell. Shi-An's revenge gradually becomes an inquiry into why the imperial court fears his birth name.

### Campaign acts

1. **The Ashes Speak** — Jade River, Red Sash clues, Shaolin and the Faceless Magistrate.
2. **Five Shadows at Your Back** — recruit a full party and navigate an escort-house war.
3. **Tournament of Ten Thousand Hands** — enter a multi-school tournament to identify the missing sword.
4. **The Emperor's Empty Seal** — infiltrate court networks through merchants, opera spies and magistrates.
5. **War Beneath the Monasteries** — prevent manufactured conflict between Shaolin, Wu Dang and Emei.
6. **Return of the Green Dragon Master** — decide whether vengeance, justice or a new school defines Shi-An's legacy.

## Core loops

### Exploration loop

Travel → talk and investigate → accept missions → trade/train/recruit → encounter rivals → duel → receive clues and resources → unlock routes.

### Combat loop

Read distance → use movement and defence → commit to punch, kick or weapon timing → build Chi through clean combinations → spend Chi on a style technique → call a party assist when the match turns.

### Character growth

- Level, maximum health, Chi, power, defence and speed.
- Learn styles from masters after narrative, reputation and combat requirements.
- Separate weapon mastery for staff, straight sword, saber, spear and flexible weapons.
- Party loyalty quests unlock stronger assists and alternate endings.

## Factions and reputation

Every major school has values rather than a simple good/evil axis. Shaolin respects discipline, Wu Dang respects restraint, Beggar Kings respect generosity, escort houses respect fulfilled promises, merchants respect profitable reliability, and outlaw schools respect courage. Choices can earn one faction's trust while making another suspicious.

## Content model

The game ships with structured archives for named fighters, schools, styles, weapons and locations. Production maps reference these records rather than generating anonymous filler at runtime. Named records can therefore recur in rumours, tournament brackets, escort missions, bounties and faction conflicts.

## Visual direction

- Detailed 16-bit-inspired pixel art with modern lighting and readability.
- Dark jade, lacquer black, aged gold, parchment and crimson.
- Clear silhouettes, oversized hand-authored impact frames and green Chi effects.
- Portraits use painterly pixel rendering; world sprites remain crisp at integer-like scale.
- UI resembles carved wood, bronze fittings and inked travel journals.

## Audio direction

Melodic chiptune instrumentation evokes bamboo flute, plucked strings, drums and temple gongs without sampling copyrighted recordings. Combat SFX emphasize cloth snaps, wood impact, steel arcs and short Chi bursts.
